UC GIS Week 2020

November 17th, 18th, and 19th

Conference Details

The UC GIS Week committee is proud to announce the first UC-Wide Gis Week. Join us for a 3-day virtual conference which celebrates the GIS work of the University of California faculty, staff, and students. The University of California System is holding an inaugural UC GIS Week on November 17th – 19th, 2020. We will celebrate all things mapping and geospatial. In light of the challenging times, we are coming together virtually to share our work with our community. If you are a researcher, student, industry partner, alumnus, or community mapper, there is space for you to share your accomplishments and inspire others through your mapping.

Tuesday November 17, 2020

Time Zone: PST/Pacific Time (US & Canada)

Workshops

10:00-2:00
Un taller introductorio de QGIS para principiantes (Spanish language workshop) (Registration is full but the workshop will be streamed to YouTube)

Alaina Mallette

This introductory session will cover the basic concepts of geographic information systems, including the five principal components: hardware, software, data, process/methods, and people. An overview of the essential hardware and the most common software will be presented. This session will use publicly-accessible data sources and explain common data collection processes, including mobile data collection and field worksheets. There will be hands-on modules for skill-building and practice on these topics:

  • Data visualization
  • Layer properties
  • Basic tools
  • Attribute tables
  • Spatial and non-spatial data queries
  • Geoprocessing tools and processing toolbox
  • Plugins
  • Geographic design and layout manager

The session will end with a dialogue on leveraging individual and organizational networks for a more productive and effective geographic information system, using examples from Mexico, Ukraine, and New York.

Wednesday November 18, 2020

Time Zone: PST/Pacific Time (US & Canada)

Workshop

10:00-1:00
Visualizing 2D Geology with QGIS (Registration is full but the workshop will be streamed to YouTube)

Diane Fritz

Have you ever been curious about how the rock formations you see when you’re hiking through a varying landscape look underground? This workshop will give you an introduction to satisfying that curiosity with QGIS! We’ll discuss some basic geologic principles, play with a symbol library made specifically for geologic maps and learn how to make estimated geologic cross sections with the qProf plugin. At the end, we’ll touch on finishing off our cross section in the vector graphics software Inkscape.

We’ll step through a sample location together with provided data, but I’ll give you some resources to find your own data to build a geologic representations of your area of interest.

Time Zone: PST/Pacific Time (US & Canada)

Workshop

10:00-12:00
Collecting data using Input and QGIS (Registration is full but the workshop will be streamed to YouTube)

Lutra Consulting

Using QGIS and Input, users will set up and configure their project to collect data in the field. Users will need a PC/laptop with QGIS (3.14) and a mobile/tablet device.

The following topics will be covered during the workshop:

  • Preparing QGIS project for survey:
    • Setting up background layers for online/offline (XYZ tile, vector tiles, etc)
    • Configuring forms
  • Sharing project and inviting collaborators to the survey project
  • Using Input
  • Syncing data, track changes, history logs, etc.
